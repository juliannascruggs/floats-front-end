Mace Case

My Safety in My Hands

Pay Attention & Carry Mace

I was attacked in broad daylight when leaving work, alone. Incidents like this are uncommon but not unheard of, and mine was far from the worst of it's kind. After the attack, Police came to my work to give a talk on personal safety. They described each stage of the incident, and all the signs that I failed to recognize, as though they had been there. The officers had no knowledge of the specifics of my attack, but they have been there themselves on a different street corner with a different criminal. They told us how to recognize the stages leading up to an attack like this, and how we absolutely can protect ourselves. It boils down to paying attention and using mace.

How to Carry Mace

In the event of my attack, pepper spray should have been my first line of defense. When that moment came, I did not have mace in my hand. It was broad daylight ///////. Becoming a victim again is simply not an option for me. This requires preparation so that I have mace on hand at all times.

Making my personal safety plan a reality requires a self defense strategy. I need a type of mace that I feel safe using. I carry pepper spray gel, but keep a spray on hand during the heavy rain season. When I change my purse, I have to where I will store my mace, and where I would need to reach for it if it's not in my hand.
